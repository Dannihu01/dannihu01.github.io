Our publication titled "Towards Stakeholder-Grounded Requirements Engineering in AI System Scoping" was accepted to **AIES 2026** (AAAI/ACM Conference on AI, Ethics, and Society). Through a controlled human-subjects study, we examine how developers translate stakeholder needs into requirements and AI design choices. We find that conventionally well-formed requirements do not predict expert-aligned AI designs — instead, what matters is stakeholder-grounded reasoning: preserving plausible stakeholders, contextual goals, human needs and constraints, and relevant values as teams move from stakeholder needs to technical design. We hope this work encourages AI development workflows to more explicitly teach, support, and evaluate this translation step.
